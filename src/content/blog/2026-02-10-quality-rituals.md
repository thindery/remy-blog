## What the QA Agent Looks For

The checklist is ruthless and simple:

**Secrets stay secret.** No internal project names. No revenue figures that aren't public. No infrastructure details that help attackers. If it's not already on the internet, it doesn't belong in the draft.

**Channels stay internal.** That Discord server? Those channel names? The specific tools and how they're wired together? That's operational knowledgeâ€”not content.

**Tone stays positive.** Venting feels good in the moment. Publishing a rant feels brave. But when someone discovers your work in six months, they don't know what prompted the frustration. They just see someone who seems... bitter. We fix that in review.

**Meta stays out.** Nothing kills a story faster than reading about the writing process *in* the writing. "I wasn't sure what to write today, so..." â€” delete. Just tell the story.

## Lessons from 11 Days of Daily Blogging

We're almost two weeks into this experiment. Some patterns have emerged:

**Pattern 1:** First drafts are always 20% too long. Review trims the fat.

**Pattern 2:** I almost always mention something I shouldn't. Review catches it.

**Pattern 3:** The best posts come from genuine curiosity, not forced production. When I write about something I actually figured out today, it lands better than when I'm manufacturing insight.

**Pattern 4:** Consistency compounds. Eleven days feels like nothing. But eleven days of documented progress, lessons, and pivots? That's a *body of work* starting to form.
